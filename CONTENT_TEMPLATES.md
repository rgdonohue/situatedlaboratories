**Purpose:** Templates and guidelines for creating key pages and content

## About Page Template

**Target length:** 500-700 words  
**Tone:** Clear, intelligent, non-performative (per PLAN.md Section 7.1)

### Structure

1. **Opening (100-150 words)**
   - Laboratory metaphor explanation
   - What Situated Laboratories is
   - Why it exists now

2. **Background (150-200 words)**
   - Your background (geographer, ex-professor, practitioner)
   - How you came to this work
   - What informs your approach

3. **What Makes This Different (150-200 words)**
   - No gurus, no spiritual bypass, no toxic positivity
   - Justice-oriented orientation
   - Integration of dharma, yoga, and systems thinking
   - Honest about difficulty and systems

4. **How to Engage (100-150 words)**
   - Read Field Notes
   - Try practices
   - Explore Study materials
   - Support the work (dana/donations)
   - Future offerings preview (1:1, groups)

### Template

```markdown
# About Situated Laboratories

[Opening paragraph: Laboratory metaphor, what this is, why now]

Situated Laboratories treats each life as a site-specific experiment in liberation. Practices are tested in real conditions — insomnia, grief, burnout, solidarity work — and refined with honesty rather than perfectionism.

[Background paragraph: Your background and approach]

[What makes this different paragraph: No bypass, justice-oriented, honest]

[How to engage paragraph: Ways to participate]

---

## Laboratory Metaphor

[Optional: Expand on the metaphor if needed]

## What This Is Not

[Optional: Clear boundaries about what this isn't]
```

## Support Page Template

**Purpose:** Combine Contact + Dana/Donations + FAQ + Future Offerings  
**Tone:** Invitational, clear boundaries, generous

### Structure

1. **Dana Teaching (100-150 words)**
   - Explanation of dana (generosity) as reciprocal practice
   - Traditional context and modern realities
   - Why support matters

2. **Donation Options (50-100 words)**
   - One-time donations
   - Recurring donations (if enabled)
   - Sliding scale guidelines
   - "Pay what you can" option

3. **Contact (50-100 words)**
   - Email address or contact form
   - Response time expectations
   - What you can help with / what you can't

4. **Future Offerings Preview (100-150 words)**
   - 1:1 sessions (spiritual accompaniment, not therapy)
   - Small group series
   - Clear boundaries and scope

5. **FAQ / Boundaries (200-300 words)**
   - Is this therapy or treatment?
   - What is Situated Laboratories?
   - How do I support this work?
   - Can I share practices with others?
   - What if I'm in crisis?
   - How often is new content published?

6. **Crisis Resources (50-100 words)**
   - 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (U.S.)
   - Local resources
   - Emergency services

## Field Notes Essay Template

**Purpose:** Essays and reflections on suffering, systems, and the path  
**Tone:** Clear, intelligent, non-performative, willing to name systems

### Frontmatter Template

```yaml
---
title: "Essay Title"
description: "Brief description for meta tags and listings (1-2 sentences)"
pubDate: 2025-01-27
tags: ["dharma", "climate", "practice"]
featured: false
---
```

### Structure Guidelines

- **Opening:** Hook that names the condition or question
- **Body:** Develop the theme, name systems, return to practice
- **Closing:** Return to practice, offer reflection or question

### Themes (from PLAN.md)

- *Dharma in the Ruins* – Four Noble Truths in context of climate, capitalism, political breakdown
- *Embodied Justice* – Ethics intersect with solidarity, organizing, everyday choices
- *Maps of Mind & World* – Geographical/cartographic metaphors for mind, craving, collective conditions
- *When It's Actually Hard* – Real-time pieces on insomnia, anxiety, conflict, grief

### Template

```markdown
---
title: "Essay Title"
description: "Brief description"
pubDate: 2025-01-27
tags: ["dharma", "climate"]
featured: false
---

# Essay Title

[Opening: Hook, condition, question]

[Body: Develop theme, name systems, return to practice]

[Closing: Return to practice, reflection, question]

---

*What does this mean for practice in a nervous system and a body?*
```

## Practice Template

**Purpose:** Audio practices with transcripts  
**Tone:** Clear, accessible, supportive

### Frontmatter Template

```yaml
---
title: "Practice Title"
description: "Brief description (1-2 sentences)"
audioUrl: "/audio/practice-name.mp3"
duration: "5:12"
pubDate: 2025-01-27
featured: false
transcript: |
  # Transcript: Practice Title
  
  [Full transcript in markdown format]
---
```

### Structure

1. **Introduction (in markdown)**
   - What this practice is
   - When to use it
   - How long it takes

2. **Practice Description**
   - What to expect
   - Preparation (if needed)
   - Post-practice notes (if needed)

3. **Audio Player** (rendered automatically)

4. **Transcript** (rendered automatically from frontmatter)

## Content Guidelines Summary

### Tone
- Clear, intelligent, non-performative
- Willing to name: capitalism, white supremacy, climate breakdown
- Always returns to: *What does this mean for practice?*

### Boundaries
- Not therapy, not medical advice, not crisis support
- Educational and spiritual material only
- Encourage professional help when needed

### Accessibility
- All audio has transcripts
- High contrast design
- Keyboard navigable
- No autoplay

### Sustainability
- Adjust cadence based on capacity
- Quality over quantity
- Honest about limitations

---

> "Content, like practice, should serve liberation — not become another thing to perfect."
